PART B - PASSWORD SECURITY REVIEW
_______________________________________________

1. What makes a strong password?

A strong password contains uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and special characters. It should be at least 12 characters long and difficult to guess.

2. Why should passwords be unique?

Unique passwords prevent attackers from accessing multiple accounts if one password is compromised.

3. What is Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)?

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) is a security method that requires two or more verification methods before granting access to an account.

4. What are the risks of password reuse?

- Multiple accounts can be compromised.
- Personal information may be stolen.
- Identity theft can occur.
- Financial loss may occur.

Password Policy

1. Use at least 12 characters.
2. Include uppercase and lowercase letters.
3. Include numbers and special symbols.
4. Avoid personal information.
5. Enable MFA whenever possible.
6. Change passwords regularly.
7. Never share passwords.

PART D - CYBER THREAT RESEARCH
_______________________________________________

1. Phishing

Definition:
Phishing is a cyber attack where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information through fake emails, messages, or websites.

Real-World Example:
A fake bank email asks users to verify account details.

Prevention Methods:
- Verify sender information.
- Avoid suspicious links.
- Enable MFA.
- Use antivirus software.

-------------------------------------

2. Malware

Definition:
Malware is malicious software designed to damage systems or steal information.

Real-World Example:
Downloading infected software from untrusted websites.

Prevention Methods:
- Use antivirus software.
- Avoid unknown downloads.
- Keep software updated.

--------------------------------------------------

3. Ransomware

Definition:
Ransomware encrypts files and demands payment to restore access.

Real-World Example:
The WannaCry ransomware attack affected thousands of systems worldwide.

Prevention Methods:
- Keep backups.
- Update software regularly.
- Avoid suspicious attachments.

--------------------------------------------------

4. Social Engineering

Definition:
Social engineering manipulates people into revealing confidential information.

Real-World Example:
An attacker pretends to be technical support and requests login credentials.

Prevention Methods:
- Verify identities.
- Never share passwords.
- Follow security policies.

--------------------------------------------------

5. Data Breach

Definition:
A data breach occurs when sensitive information is accessed without authorization.

Real-World Example:
Customer records leaked from a company database.

Prevention Methods:
- Use strong passwords.
- Encrypt sensitive data.
- Enable MFA.
- Conduct security audits.
